Where Should You Go During Your Trip To Tokyo?

1.Akihabara District

A district in central Tokyo that is famous for its many electronic shops. In more recent years it has gained recognition as the center of Japan's otaku (diehard fan) culture. Many shops devoted to anime and manga are now dispersed among the electronic stores in the district. Akihabara is a place full of fun people of all ages.

2.Asakusa

A district in Tokyo where an atmosphere of Tokyo's past decades survives. For many centuries, Asakusa used to be Tokyo's leading entertainment district. In the late 1800s and early 1900s, modern types of entertainment, including movie theaters, set foot in Asakusa. There are a handful of temples and shrines including the main attraction of Sensoji, a very popular Buddhist temple built in the 7th century. Nakamise is a shopping street that provides visitors with a variety of traditional snacks and souvenirs. This is another spot that can be enjoyed for all ages.

3.Shibuya

One of Tokyo's most busy districts, packed with shopping, dining, and nightclubs. It is a center of youth and fashion culture, and its streets are the birthplace to many of Japan's fashion and entertainment trends. Over a dozen major department store branches can be found around the area. If any of the age 20+ crowd wants to enjoy the nightlife, there are a number of casually styled clubs to hit so you don't even have to worry about dressing up.
